CERTIFICATION PROCESS

Overview of the PERM Process

The PERM Labor Certification process, overseen by the U.S. Department of Labor, helps employers access foreign labor when no qualified U.S. workers are available. DS World Consulting handles the entire process efficiently, ensuring compliance and success.

Step 1: Prevailing Wage Determination

The first step is requesting a Prevailing Wage Determination from the DOL, ensuring that overseas candidates are paid at least the local prevailing wage or the employer's entry-level wage. This review, based on job duties and location, typically takes about four months.

Step 2: Recruitment Process

Simultaneously with the prevailing wage request, we assist employers with the Recruitment process to show a local labor shortage. This involves advertising job openings, reviewing applicants, and preparing a Recruitment Report. The process typically takes 60 days and is conducted alongside the prevailing wage determination to expedite the overall timeline.

Step 3: File Labor Certification

After completing recruitment and receiving the prevailing wage, we file the Permanent Foreign Labor Certification with the DOL. This straightforward application includes job details, recruitment steps, and candidate information. The process typically takes 4 months, though some cases may require additional documentation if selected for audit.

Step 4: Candidate Immigration Process

Once the labor certification is approved, we submit the case to USCIS, then to the National Visa Center for document collection. After background checks and an interview, candidates are issued a visa to move to the U.S. The entire process takes approximately 4 to 8 months.

A Solution for Staffing Challenges

While the process is not fast and involves multiple sequential steps, it offers a solution for employers struggling with staffing shortages by accessing a global pool of qualified labor.
