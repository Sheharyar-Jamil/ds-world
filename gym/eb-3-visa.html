PERM/EB-3

Sponsorship for Workers and Families

U.S. businesses can sponsor workers and their families for permanent residence, whether from abroad or already in the U.S. DS World Consulting has extensive experience guiding both employers and workers through this process.

Filing the PERM Application for Employees

The process begins when we file a PERM (Form ETA-9089) on behalf of the employer, listing the employee as the beneficiary. The U.S. Department of Labor certifies the position, which may require responding to an audit for additional documentation on recruitment efforts.

Filing the I-140 Petition

Upon successful certification of the position, our office will next file a Petition for Immigrant Worker, called a “Form I-140,” to U.S. Citizenship & Immigration Services. This requires providing USCIS with the formal labor certification as well as additional information about the employer.

Process for Workers Inside vs. Outside the U.S.

The process diverges based on location: If the worker is in the U.S., we file for Adjustment of Status with the I-140. If outside the U.S., we wait for I-140 approval, then proceed with document collection, background checks, and an immigrant visa interview at the U.S. embassy.
